\section*{Welcome to York}

\textbf{HackSoc} is the student-run society for Computer Science
students and enthusiasts. We're open to all members of the university,
but we're more likely to be something \textit{you're} interested in,
so welcome, potential member!

We run a lot of events throughout the term, and are always trying out
new things. This term we're excited to have an operating systems
course, aimed at people with no knowledge of C, which will take you
all the way to writing a little multi-tasking operating system for an
embedded processor! We also have fortnightly talks, two of which will
be given by invited speakers next term, and two socials a week. All of
our events are open to anyone, so feel free to come along to anything
which takes your fancy.

Why not get to know us before term starts? We're on Facebook, Twitter,
and IRC. If you've never used IRC before, we have a little guide at
\textbf{hacksoc.org/irc.html}

See you next term.

\section*{What's on?}

\event{Talks}{Every other Tuesday evening}

In which we sit in a room and listen to someone share their
wisdom. Anyone can give a talk, and generally they're quite
interesting. This term we're expecting two external speakers: schedule
will be announced at the start of term.

\vspace{0.1cm}

\event{OSDev Course}{Every Wednesday afternoon}

Know C? No? Well, this is a perfect chance to learn! Over a period of
7 weeks, go from the absolute basics to writing your very own
multitasking OS for an embedded system.

\vspace{0.1cm}

\event{Game Jam}{Friday Week 4}

We're going to try and make a game (at least one) in an evening! This
is largely intended to prepare people for Ludum Dare, a weekend-long
game making competition, later in the year.

\vspace{0.1cm}

\subsection*{Regular Socials}

We also have a lot of socials: when we're not doing talks, we're going
to the pub, every other Friday we're playing boardgames and eating
cake, and every Wednesday we're having lunch in the Glasshouse
(Langwith College bar).
